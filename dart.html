Dartmoor Ponies are the native pony breed of the county of Devon in the South West of England.
Its scientific name is Equus ferus caballus
Breeds are no taller then 127 cm, and can come in the colours bay, brown, black, grey, chestnut, or roan.
The earliest reference of the breed occured in 1012, when Bishop Aelfwold of Creidton listed a Dartmoor Pony in his will.
The destruction caused by World War II diminished the number of these ponies, nearly leading to their extinction.
In 1988, the Dartmoor Pony Moorland Scheme was established by the Duchy of Cornwall and the Dartmoor Pony Society.
According to the Dartmoor National Park Authority, there are fewer than 3,000 ponies found on the moor today, from approximately 30,000 in 1950.
Although Exmoor ponies live fairly close geographically and their markings are somewhat similar, evidence now suggests that Dartmoor ponies and Exmoor ponies are not related as was once thought.
