Ponies (Equus ferus caballus) are small horses that come in a variety of different sizes and colours. There are over two hundred breeds of ponies, and they have thicker tails and mains than regular sized horses. Ponies also have two blind spots.

Examples of ponies include:

Shetland Ponies
Batak Ponies
Connemara Ponies

Also...

Ponies have thicker maines and tails than horses.
Miniature horses are smaller than ponies.
Young ponies are known as foals.
They generally have a stocky body, dense bone, round shape and well-sprung ribs.
