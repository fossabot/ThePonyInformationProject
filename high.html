The Highland Pony is a strong, well balanced pony breed with all its features being in proportion to its body.
Their height ranges from 1.32-1.44m
The most colours include grey, brown, black, bay (brown with a black points) and chestnut.
Majority also have primitive markings, such as a dorsal stripe and zebra markings on the legs.
On the whole, they have short fine-featured head with a broad forehead, muscled arched neck and short legs with light feathering. Many Highland Ponies have zebra markings, and the animals themselves are native to the Scottish Highlands.
Their average lifespan is 25-30 years, and they were never purposely cross-bred to other animals to create a type.
The Highland Pony Society was formed in 1923, and the pony is currently listed as 'at risk' by the Rare Breeds Survival Trust.
Highland Ponies are often used for forestry work, they they have also been used for trekking, driving, for removing deer carcases, and for children.
It is the largest and strongest of all the native pony breeds in Great Britain, and today, there are an estimated 5,500 Highlands in the world, with most in Europe.
